## What is a Pivotal Point?

A KEY PRICE LEVEL where the stock must make a decision - break out or reverse.

**How to identify them:**
- **Round numbers:** $50, $100, $200 (psychological levels)
- **Previous highs/lows:** Resistance that failed becomes pivotal
- **Consolidation boundaries:** After sideways trading, the range edges become pivotal

## The Livermore Pattern (The Same Pattern We Hunt)

```
CONSOLIDATION (stock trades sideways)
         ‚Üì
BREAKOUT (price breaks above/below range)
         ‚Üì
CONFIRMATION (first few bars move in breakout direction + VOLUME increases)
         ‚Üì
NORMAL REACTION (pullback on LOWER volume)
         ‚Üì
TREND RESUMES (volume increases again, trend continues)
```

**If any step fails = EXIT**

| STEP | WHAT TO DO |
